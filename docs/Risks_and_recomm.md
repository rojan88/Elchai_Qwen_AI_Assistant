# Risks and Recommendation

## Risks
### Privacy

Business documents may contain confidential information. Sensitive data should remain within secure company infrastructure, and local deployment should be preferred whenever possible.

--

### Security

The AI system should operate under appropriate access controls. Users should only be allowed to process documents they are authorized to access.

---

### Model Accuracy

Although Qwen performs well, it can occasionally generate incorrect or incomplete responses. All outputs should be reviewed by employees before use

---

### Hallucination Risk

Like other Large Language Models, Qwen may confidently produce inaccurate information. Human verification is recommended to the important business decisions.

--

### Hardware Requirements

Larger versions of the model may require GPUs with significant VRAM or high-memory systems for efficient local deployment.

---

### Integration Complexity

Connecting AI assistants with existing ERP, CRM, or internal databases requires additional development, testing, and security validation.

---

### Reliability

AI-generated responses may vary depending on prompt quality, document structure, and input clarity.

---

# Final Recommendation

The project demonstrates that Qwen is suitable for internal productivity improvements.

Recommended approach:

- Start with a limited internal pilot.
- use AI for document summarization and drafting tasks.
- Keep human approval for all business-critical decisions.
- Monitor performance and collect employee feedback before wider deployment.

## Recommendation Status

**Proceed with Limited Internal Testing**

This approach minimizes risk while allowing Elchai Group to evaluate the practical value of open-source AI in real business workflows.
